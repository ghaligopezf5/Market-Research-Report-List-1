<p><p>The anti-hyperlipidemic agents market encompasses various drug classes aimed at managing dyslipidemia. Statins effectively lower LDL cholesterol by inhibiting HMG-CoA reductase. Fibrates primarily reduce triglycerides and raise HDL cholesterol levels. Niacin, a B vitamin, improves lipid profiles but may have side effects. Bile acid sequestrants bind bile acids to reduce cholesterol absorption, while cholesterol absorption inhibitors block dietary cholesterol absorption in the intestines. Each class offers distinct mechanisms, catering to diverse patient needs and treatment strategies.</p></p>
