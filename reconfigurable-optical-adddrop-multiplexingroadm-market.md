<p><p>Reconfigurable Optical Add-Drop Multiplexing (ROADM) is a technology that enables flexible management of wavelength-division multiplexed (WDM) optical networks. It allows for the dynamic addition and drop of specific wavelengths at different points in a network without needing to regenerate the entire optical signal. This capability enhances network efficiency, optimizes bandwidth usage, and reduces operational costs, making it particularly valuable in the context of increasing data traffic and the demand for high-speed communication.</p><p>The ROADM market is projected to witness significant growth, driven by the surging demand for high-capacity networks owing to cloud computing, video streaming, and IoT applications.
