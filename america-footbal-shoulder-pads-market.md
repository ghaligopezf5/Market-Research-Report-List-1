<p><p>The American football shoulder pads market is divided into two primary types: cantilevered and noncantilevered. Cantilevered shoulder pads feature an added structure that extends outward, providing superior protection and support by distributing impact forces away from the player's body. They are commonly used in contact positions, like linebackers. In contrast, noncantilevered shoulder pads are more streamlined and closer to the body, allowing for greater mobility and flexibility, making them ideal for skill positions like wide receivers. Both types cater to diverse player needs and preferences.</p></p>
