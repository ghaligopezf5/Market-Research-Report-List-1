<p><p>Controlled-release drug delivery systems are designed to optimize therapeutic effects by releasing drugs at a controlled rate over time. The Wurster technique involves spray-drying to create coated drug particles. Coacervation uses polymer solutions to encapsulate drugs. Microencapsulation protects active ingredients while controlling release. Implants provide sustained drug release directly into the body. Transdermal systems allow drug penetration through the skin. Targeted delivery focuses on specific tissues or cells to minimize side effects. Other methods include liposomes and hydrogels, enhancing versatility in treatment options.</p></p>
